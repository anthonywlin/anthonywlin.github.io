On Unary-conjunctive-view Queries (Honours Thesis)

Author: Anthony Widjaja To

Abstract:

We study the computational behavior and the expressive power of the unary-conjunctive-view (UCV) query language, which is an extension of the Lowenheim class (i.e. monadic first-order logic) without equality. We show that the UCV language satisfies the finite model property, i.e. every satisfiable UCV query has a finite model. This immediately implies decidability for the UCV language. Furthermore, we showed that satisfiability for the UCV language is in 2-NEXPTIME but is NEXPTIME-hard. We, then, proceed by deriving a general method for proving inexpressibility results for the UCV language. Our method makes use of the homomorphism preservation theorem of model theory. Then, we tap into our general methodology theorem to derive numerous inexpressibility results for the UCV language. In particular, we show that the following generic queries are not expressible in the UCV language over all finite graphs: ``the existence of a k-path'' (k >= 1), ``the existence of a k-cycle'' (k >= 2), ``the existence of a k-clique'' (k >= 2), ``that the graph is undirected'', and ``the edge relation is transitive''. We also derive a similar result when we restrict the ground set to `all finite undirected loopless graphs', except that this time the generic query ``the existence of a k-clique'' (k >= 1) is expressible in the language. Despite the inexpressibility results derived in this report, we prove that the UCV language is closed under boolean operations and first-order quantifications - a property rarely satisfied by the known decidable fragments of first-order logic that have formulas over any vocabulary. Finally, we compare the UCV language with the finite variable logic. Roughly speaking, our results stipulate that neither the UCV language nor the k-variable logic (k >= 2) is more expressive than the other. Interestingly, we show that the 1-variable logic is strictly less expressive than the UCV language.
