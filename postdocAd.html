One Postdoc Position Available

One postdoctoral research fellowship is available at Yale-NUS College, Singapore, on the topic of String Constraints for Vulnerability Analysis in Web Applications. Applicants should have expertise in two or more of the following fields:

  • Software verification/program analysis.
  • Web security.
  • Logic and automata

The project will focus on both foundational issues (including decidability, complexity), and implementation issues (including benchmarking, and heuristics for optimising string solvers).

Applications will be assessed on a first-come, first-served basis. Initial appointment is for one year and will be extended upon satisfactory progress.

Please email Dr. Anthony Widjaja Lin for more information. Enquiries and expression of interests are welcome.

Sample Publications:

  • Anthony W. Lin and Pablo Barcelo. String Solving with Word Equations and Transducers: Towards a Logic for Analysing Mutation XSS. In POPL 2016. [Click here for my slides for the conference talk.]

About Working at Yale-NUS

The research will be conducted at Yale-NUS College. Yale-NUS College is a small liberal arts college located within National University of Singapore (NUS). An appointed research fellow at Yale-NUS will also be an employee of NUS and enjoy NUS generous employee benefits package. The successful candidate will be collaborating with the PI of the project (Dr. Anthony W. Lin), and expected to supervise a couple of interns working on the project. He/she will also have the opportunity to interact with collaborators on the project including Dr. Philipp Ruemmer (Uppsala University, Sweden) and Dr. Prateek Saxena (National University of Singapore).
